DNC security plan released

Posted by Corey Conner on Aug 9, 2012 | 0 comments

Home » Blog » DNC security plan released

Photo Courtesy of Willamor Media

From Sunday, Sept. 2, through Friday, Sept. 7, security for the DNC will limit pedestrian and vehicular traffic in the uptown area. Packard Place, The PPL’s home at Church and 3rd Street will remain fully accessible despite its location near the convention’s main venues.

According to the Charlotte Observer, uptown businesses, along with the city, are working to ensure traffic disruptions have a minimal impact on day-to-day operations.

“Folks that want to stay open will be able to stay open,” said Charlotte Mayor Anthony Foxx, a Democrat.

…

While Tryon Street will be open during much of the DNC, it will become a one-way street for northbound traffic between Stonewall and 12th streets from 6 a.m. Tuesday, Sept. 4, through Wednesday, Sept. 5. Most east-west streets crossing it in uptown will be closed.

That could make it difficult for commuters to access large parking garages used by office workers in city office buildings.

At Bank of America, which employs about 15,000 people in Charlotte, each department has made plans to keep business activities running, spokesman Scott Silvestri said. That includes encouraging employees to work from a mix of uptown offices, alternate bank facilities and their homes, he said.

A one block area around Time Warner Cable Arena will only be accessible to those with credentials. People working uptown, outside of the areas requiring credentials, may have to deal with security checkpoints to reach limited-access areas.

However, people may not be able to park in their typical parking garage.

Potentially the biggest traffic disruption from the convention will come at the end of the week. The John Belk Freeway will be closed on Thursday near the Bank of America Stadium in preparation for President Obama’s acceptance speech that night.

Following Obama’s speech and the conclusion of the convention, locals can expect a return to normalcy after 9AM on Friday morning.

The Charlotte Observer has more on traffic and pedestrian disruption during the DNC.

Convention Activities

Although the convention does not officially begin until the 3rd, other events will disrupt traffic starting on Sept. 2. The Coalition to March on Wall Street South will march in their “accessible, family-friendly protest”, prompting road closures along the route.

The group will gather at Frazier Park and march pass Bank of America’s headquarters, Wells-Fargo’s east coast headquarters, and the Duke Energy Center.

On Monday, the DNC will kick off the convention with CarolinaFest, the uptown event originally planned for Charlotte Motor Speedway. CarolinaFest will take place along Tryon Street between Trade and Stonewall, making that section of Tryon inaccessible to vehicles. Tryon will remain open to pedestrians throughout the week.

According to the organizing committee,

CarolinaFest is a family friendly celebration that will bring more Americans than ever before directly into the heart of convention activity, including an opportunity to witness delegate caucus meetings that will occur in Uptown. The free event will feature interactive expositions, family oriented activities, live music, and speakers.
