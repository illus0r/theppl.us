An Exciting Change Brings CarolinaFest 2012 to Uptown Charlotte

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Jun 26, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Photo Property of CLT BLog

The Charlotte in 2012 convention host committee opted to move the location of CarolinaFest 2012 to Tryon Street in Uptown Charlotte. The committee had originally decided to hold the event in the Charlotte Motor Speedway this year but they believe this new change better meets the needs of the community.

Dan Murray, the executive director of the charlotte in 2012 host committee, listed a few of the benefits this new change will bring, during a press conference earlier this afternoon. Some of those benefits include more convenient transportation options as well as access to the caucus meetings that will be taking place in nearby locations. Dan Murray also believes hosting CarolinaFest 2012 in Uptown Charlotte will help local businesses that will be open to the public during the events.

For more information, visit charlottein2012.com

Occupy movement looks toward political conventions

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Apr 25, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Chuck Burton/Associated Press

In planning protests this time around, for the conventions, social media is likely to play a bigger role.

“I think there’s been a lot more inter-occupation communication as people got away from the encampments and starting getting online and establishing lines of communication across the country,” said Domenic Battistella, 34, of nearby Mooresville, an original member of Occupy Charlotte. “We’re going to be much more coordinated on a regional basis.”

Occupy Charlotte members recently met with Occupy protesters from throughout North Carolina to prepare for the convention and are planning to provide housing, food and other support for those who come from out of state. An Occupy Durham member is now helping the legal team in a potential challenge as Charlotte officials continue to hold off allowing groups to apply for permits to protest at the convention.

Michael Zytkow, 25, an Occupy Charlotte member (…) hopes [an upcoming Bank of America] protest draws as many as 1,000 people, providing a test run for the convention and the city’s ability to control the demonstrations.

And how many will come for the convention?

“I’m guessing in the thousands upon thousands,” Mr. Zytkow said.

Read more by Viv Bernstein at the New York Times.

New documentary #whilewewatch explores citizen media and Occupy Wall Street

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Feb 20, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Watch the trailer:

Filmmaker Kevin Breslin presents #whilewewatch, a film about the use of social, citizen media during the rise of #OccupyWallSt.

Ruben Sun from PSFK sums it up:

The film followed key events of Occupy Wall Street including the Brooklyn Bridge march on October 1st, “Pepper Spray Cop,” the Global Day of Action on October 15th and Occupy’s eviction on November 17th. Also captured were many forms of self-organizing mechanisms of the movement such as live streamers, globalrevolution.tv media hq and wearethe99percent.tumblr.com. Finally, the film highlighted how the movement managed to stay instep with its overall message despite the diverse perspectives of its followers.

Find out more about the film on the official website, and on Twitter and Facebook.
