Media partnership with Netroots Nation!

Posted by Desiree Kane on Aug 6, 2012 in Press Releases | 1 comment

Charlotte, NC

The Democratic National Convention is less than a month away and as it draws near, news of progress and positive change is afoot.

Charlotte-based project The PPL (pronounced “The People”)—one aimed at providing a platform, stage programming, coworking space and resources for the global unaccredited media producers in town for the convention—is partnering up with Netroots Foundation, a nonprofit that brings together online citizens across America, injects progressive voices into the national conversation and advances the values of justice, equality and community in our nation’s politics. It’s this kind of joint effort that exemplifies tangible momentum happening right now in North Carolina’s largest city.

Through this partnership, both organizations will serve a single goal: to increase the level of discourse present in today’s political system through empowering new media makers of all sorts. Packard Place, the location of The PPL events in September, will house these resources, including a stage with programming aimed at connecting decision makers in Washington with the online progressive community.

The PPL’s main stage content is being organized around the four legacy items of the DNCC: healthy children/healthy families, youth employment and civic engagement,  building a broader, more inclusive society, and  energy/tech/sustainability. Democracy for America will also host a special broadcast studio that will be the hub for creating and distributing content about progressive strategy for 2012 and beyond. This content will be streamed at NetrootsNation.org and online, as well as shared with partner organizations around the country.

The volunteer base is ramping up support for The PPL as well. Queens University is staffing a large majority of the volunteer positions and alongside them will be Progress NC, an affiliate of Progress Now.

Netroots Foundation, which organizes the largest annual progressive conference Netroots Nation, was also a major partner in the successful Big Tent event at the 2008 Democratic National Convention in Denver, with more than 1,000 participants per day representing 200 blogs and dozens of organizations.

Press Release: Stage for the people by The PPL

Posted by Desiree Kane on Jun 25, 2012 in Press Releases | 1 comment

Stage for the people by The PPL

Public is invited to submit ideas for stage presentations during the Democratic National Convention

Charlotte, N.C. – The PPL, in an innovative move to democratize its stage, requests topic submissions for their stage during the Democratic National Convention.  The submissions will be accepted through July 3 and announced in early August.  In the spirit of extending the concept of an open and accessible convention experience for the community, The PPL’s stage is an opportunity for convention attendees to present and discuss the topics and issues through their own lens.

“The PPL’s curated conversations, which will involve thought and action leaders from our community and beyond, is way to make it possible for visiting independent media professionals to plug into important topics surrounding the DNC including ideological, economic, environmental, and social agendas.” Says Candice Langston, Stage Production Lead.

Example themes and topics sought after include:
  • the future of civic, voter, and/or engagement
  • dialogue vs. diatribe
  • the future of social media
  • open forum engagement
  • the economic divide
  • bridging ideological gaps
  • faith-based discrimination
  • exploring economic and social agendas
  • environmental sustainability
  • connecting the dots
  • healthcare reform
  • interconnectedness
  • higher education reform
Submission deadline is July 3, 11:59PM.
Voting on suggested topics will begin after July 3rd and announced in early August.

The PPL is a non-partisan grassroots initiative to promote healthy, productive public discourse by providing work space and resources for bloggers and independent media in Charlotte during the DNC. Although we welcome all media personnel, flights and accommodations will not be paid for by The PPL, however we will help with any questions. CNN City Smackdown: 18 reasons on the last day to play

Posted by Desiree Kane on May 21, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Today is the last day to upload your stories to the CNN iReport City Smackdown! This is a great opportunity for anyone with a story to tell about Charlotte to get their video on national news. That’s killer media attention, why not capitalize on it?!? The only requirement is that it be about something you like about Charlotte.

I personally can think of at least 18 things/places/reasons to like Charlotte so if you’re hard up for ideas on what to make a video (to upload today!) about, here’s something to get you started:

  1. That doughnut smell Dilworth always has
  2. Freedom Park
  3. The public art gallery in NoDa
  4. Latta Plantation Park (yes, it’s technically in Huntersville)
  5. What good has become of the intersection at Freedom & W. Morehead
  6. The free CATS Gold Rush line
  7. The Ben F. Long Continuum fresco which randomly has Hugh McColl painted into it
  8. The Matheson Bridge Mural by William Puckett
  9. Lightning bugs
  10. The new Little Sugar Creek Greenway and Creek restoration
  11. Soil that is easy to grow amazing produce in
  12. That thing about Charlotte where if you really, genuinely need help someone will help you
  13. Compared to a lot of cities (not inferring anything about Tampa) Charlotte is extremely clean
  14. How almost everything is generally about 15-20 minutes away, max
  15. The big Carolina Farmers section of only local farmers and local foods at the farmer’s market on Yorkmont
  16. How we have gorgeous, pastoral views of the NC countryside 20 mins away in pretty much every direction
  17. Our blackberries in the height of the season will kick any other place’s blackberries’ booty
  18. Cool old architecture like the Excelsior Club on Beatty’s Ford, the Coca-Cola building in FreeMoreWest and Myrtle Square in Dilworth
Submit your story: http://ireport.cnn.com/topics/788285

In other, completely self serving news, here’s the Charlotte Video Project bit on what we’re up to that’s up on the CNN iReport page: http://ireport.cnn.com/docs/DOC-792138

CNN City Smack down: Bust out your phones, Charlotte!

Posted by Desiree Kane on May 16, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

In August 2010 the city rallied around promoting Charlotte as the prime spot for the #DNC2012. There was even a #DNC2012 hashtag party on Twitter and tons of people participated. There were hundreds of online tweets from people, it was simply brilliant!

The time has come to do it again, y’all! Let’s get some national media attention that we create and has our voices, places, and spaces in it!

#DNC2012 is in September!! Photo cred: Willamor Media

In the CNN City Smack down, Charlotte residents are asked show off why Charlotte is so much more awesome than Tampa, the city chosen for the #RNC2012.

CNN has already called us one of the best kept secrets in the nation. Think that’s true? We, The PPL, certainly do!

Who: You! Crown town’s finest!

What: It’s not about putting down Tampa, it’s about uplifting Charlotte!

Where: In your hand, all around you!

When: NOW! until May 21

How: Grab your phone and the next time you’re at some of your favorite spots around town, take a second to stop to show and tell everyone why Charlotte is rad! Upload it to: http://ireport.cnn.com/topics/788285

Here’s what the official CNN post says:

It’s time for a city smackdown!

As part of our Destination USA project, we’re pitting rival cities against each other. This time, it’s the rivalry of the convention cities: Charlotte, North Carolina (home of the 2012 Democratic National Convention) vs. Tampa, Florida (home of the 2012 Republican National Convention).

If you love Charlotte, here’s your chance to stand up for your city! Tell us the very best thing about it, or the one or two reasons you like it better than any other. Make sure to include your most stunning Charlotte photos to back up your claim. Show us why you can take on Tampa any day!

The best stories, photos and travel tips could be featured in a CNN story.
