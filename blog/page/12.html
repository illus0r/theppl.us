Who are these PPL?: Five questions, #3

Posted by lila on Mar 3, 2012 in Blog, Five Questions | 0 comments

The PPL is diverse, by definition: we are bloggers, photojournalists, artists and entrepreneurs of varied ages and backgrounds committed to supporting independent media during the 2012 DNC. But let’s get a little more specific.

So who are these PPL, anyway? In this five-question series, you’ll get the quick-and-dirty on the folks making this thing happen.

Name?

Justin Ruckman.

Years in Charlotte?

Born and raised, minus some college.

Claim to fame?

Started CLT Blog four years ago, which now has its own weekly TV show on WTVI

What are you looking forward to at the DNC?

I grew up here, so I love opportunities like this to see our city through the eyes of people who didn’t. Not to mention I can’t wait to hang out with all the bloggers — it’s gonna be a mini SXSW!

Why are you a part of the PPL?

This project, and online media at large, represent some of my generation’s most important values: community, transparency, diversity and the application of technology for social good. I want to see more people talking about government, and fewer people talking about politics.

Who are these PPL?: Five questions, #2

Posted by lila on Mar 2, 2012 in Blog, Five Questions | 0 comments

The PPL is diverse, by definition: we are bloggers, photojournalists, artists and entrepreneurs of varied ages and backgrounds committed to supporting independent media during the 2012 DNC. But let’s get a little more specific.

So who are these PPL, anyway? In this five-question series, you’ll get the quick-and-dirty on the folks making this thing happen.

Name?

Rhiannon Fionn-Bowman

Years in Charlotte?

I’ve lived in Charlotte since 2005. Before that I lived in Atlanta, Ga., and before that Montgomery, Ala.

Claim to fame?

Independent investigative journalist focused on Charlotte’s coal ash issues. I also covered the Jinwright tax evasion trial for Qcitymetro.com and Occupy Charlotte for Creative Loafing. I have also written on all of those topics for Charlotte magazine.

What are you looking forward to at the DNC?

I’ll be hosting a forum on coal ash for The PPL that will, if all goes as planned, bring in leaders from all sides of the issue and allow time for the community to ask questions. Coal ash is America’s second-largest waste stream and is currently unregulated at the federal level. North Carolina has more high-hazard coal ash ponds than any other state, two of which drain into Charlotte’s main drinking water reservoir.

Why are you a part of the PPL?

Because I believe the people’s voice is just as important as the voices from government and business.

Who are these PPL?: Five questions, #1

Posted by lila on Mar 2, 2012 in Blog, Five Questions | 0 comments

The PPL is diverse, by definition: we are bloggers, photojournalists, artists and entrepreneurs of varied ages and backgrounds committed to supporting independent media during the 2012 DNC. But let’s get a little more specific.

So who are these PPL, anyway? In this five-question series, you’ll get the quick-and-dirty on the folks making this thing happen.

Name?

Lila Allen.

Years in Charlotte?

All 24 so far, though technically I was in Davidson for four. That photo up there obviously isn’t Charlotte; that’s Brighton, UK.

Claim to fame?

Most people know me either through my writing (mostly at Charlotte Viewpoint or ALT CLT) or through my affiliation with art institutions in town. I’ve worked at the Mint, McColl Center, the Bechtler, and interned at Center of the Earth Gallery.

What are you looking forward to at the DNC?

I’m excited to see what local artists and curators are going to come up with for the convention — I’m ready for stuff to get weird. Since the whole country will be looking at Charlotte, I think it will give many people the incentive they need to really put themselves out there and get active and creative.

Why are you a part of the PPL?

Having grown up here, Charlotte is important to me. We’re still learning who we are as a city, and the cool thing about that is that if you want to, you can help shape it. I see the PPL as being a part of that. Getting awesome, talented and diverse people together to support indie media? Count me in. The relationships and momentum we’re building now until the DNC will continue to serve us well after the out-of-towners have left.

My role at the PPL is to help cover local arts and culture, which is something I find fun anyway. If I can put my thoughts together in a way that helps inform and entertain other people, even better.

(I’m also the lady posting these nifty interviews. See you tomorrow!)

New documentary #whilewewatch explores citizen media and Occupy Wall Street

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Feb 20, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Watch the trailer:

Filmmaker Kevin Breslin presents #whilewewatch, a film about the use of social, citizen media during the rise of #OccupyWallSt.

Ruben Sun from PSFK sums it up:

The film followed key events of Occupy Wall Street including the Brooklyn Bridge march on October 1st, “Pepper Spray Cop,” the Global Day of Action on October 15th and Occupy’s eviction on November 17th. Also captured were many forms of self-organizing mechanisms of the movement such as live streamers, globalrevolution.tv media hq and wearethe99percent.tumblr.com. Finally, the film highlighted how the movement managed to stay instep with its overall message despite the diverse perspectives of its followers.

Find out more about the film on the official website, and on Twitter and Facebook.

Yeehaw! DNC to judge best Carolina BBQ

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Feb 15, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Bidding opens today for an official barbecue sauce for the Democratic National Convention. Makers must tout the Carolinas’ three regional styles: vinegar (Eastern North Carolina), tomato-based (Western and Piedmont) and mustard-based (South Carolina.) The trio of flavors will be sold together as a gift pack on the convention website.

The winning bidder will receive a contract in an amount to be determined later, according to Jackie Bateman, grassroots finance director for the convention.

The idea is not only to show local flair, but to raise money. The local host committee is charged with raising nearly $37 million for the convention. Festivities kick off Labor Day with a festival for 35,000 at Charlotte Motor Speedway. Convention business officially starts Sept. 4, and ends Sept. 6 with President Barack Obama accepting the party’s nomination at Bank of America Stadium.

Applicants must be registered with Charlotte in 2012′s vendor directory.

Read more by Celeste Smith at the Charlotte Observer.

Zuckerberg’s S-1 letter makes bold statements about relationship between Facebook, people, and governments

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Feb 13, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

We hope to change how people relate to their governments and social institutions.

We believe building tools to help people share can bring a more honest and transparent dialogue around government that could lead to more direct empowerment of people, more accountability for officials and better solutions to some of the biggest problems of our time.

By giving people the power to share, we are starting to see people make their voices heard on a different scale from what has historically been possible. These voices will increase in number and volume. They cannot be ignored. Over time, we expect governments will become more responsive to issues and concerns raised directly by all their people rather than through intermediaries controlled by a select few.

Read his full S-1 letter here.

Jon Evans of TechCrunch noticed this language too, and speculates a bit on what he thinks it could mean going forward.

Pretty strong words from one of our generation’s greatest CEOs, and indicative of the depth of his vision for the company.
