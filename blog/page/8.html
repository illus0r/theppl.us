Learn from the Pros

Posted by jeffwilber on Jul 19, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Photo courtesy of Charlotte SPJ

 

Hone your convention coverage skills and learn how NOT to get arrested while pursuing your story!

With representatives from across the Carolinas, “Covering the DNC” is a seminar that will be hosted by the Society of Professional Journalists to teach journalists and curious individuals the proper way to cover a convention.  With topics like “Protests; How Journalists Can Avoid Getting Arrested” and “DNC Orienteering Session,” the program promises advice from convention veterans on the way the convention operates, as well as the best ways to maneuver the convention spaces for optimal coverage.

For more information, please refer to the Charlotte Chapter of the SPJ, or click through to their page:

covering-the-democratic-national-convention-in-2012

As the PPL encourages media outlets to join the activity of our events, we strongly recommend that independent media and citizen journalists take part in this seminar as preparation for the DNC.

Students: $15
SPJ Member: $15
Non-SPJ Member: $25

Register for the event at coverdnc today or are you ready to get started with The PPL? Registration is open and filling up quickly. Sign up today!

Who are these PPL: Ashley Bowen

Posted by Ashley Bowen on Jul 11, 2012 in Blog, Five Questions | 0 comments

The PPL is diverse, by definition: we are bloggers, photojournalists, artists and entrepreneurs of varied ages and backgrounds committed to supporting independent media during the 2012 DNC. But let’s get a little more specific.

So who are these PPL, anyway? In this five-question series, you’ll get the quick-and-dirty on the folks making this thing happen.


Name: Ashley Bowen (watch her video)

Years in Charlotte: 22 (all my life)

Claim to fame: Got to work with the Neighborhood Theatre for 6 months. Learned a lot about the PR/Marketing field and got to see some great shows! I love NoDa so that internship was not only great experience but fun as well!

What are you looking forward to at the DNC: I want to meet as many people as possible. I have never been a part of something as large scale as the DNC. Also, Charlotte is my hometown so I look forward to seeing the recognition this city has deserved for a long time. After Amendment One was passed people have looked down on North Carolina and Charlotte (even though was denied in Mecklenburg County.) I hope the DNC will change the negative perception of this great city!

Why are you a part of the PPL: I love this city and I hope to showcase the diversity of this city through this internship. Also, I want the community to feel more involved in the DNC. I don’t want them to think the DNC is just going to cause traffic and delays. I want them to see the economic and societal benefits the DNC will bring to the city as a whole.

Queens University’s Knight School of Communication to shut down for DNC

Posted by Ashley Bowen on Jul 10, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

We want to give a big shoutout to Queens University for donating so many volunteers during the DNC. The Knight School of Communication will shut down during the DNC leaving the doors wide open for students to donate their time. This allows students to gain real world experience without jeapordizing their studies. We, The PPL, are excited to work with more students from Queens. We have had the opportunity to work with Queens’ students before and have surely noticed their innovative education.
