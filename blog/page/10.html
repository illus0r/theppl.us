Who are these PPL: Millie Archila

Posted by Ashley Bowen on Jun 8, 2012 in Blog, Five Questions | 0 comments

The PPL is diverse, by definition: we are bloggers, photojournalists, artists and entrepreneurs of varied ages and backgrounds committed to supporting independent media during the 2012 DNC. But let’s get a little more specific.

So who are these PPL, anyway? In this five-question series, you’ll get the quick-and-dirty on the folks making this thing happen.

Name: Millie Archila (watch her video)

Years in Charlotte: This summer will be five years. I am originally from southern California.

Claim to fame: I have presented my research on human rights at Virginia Tech’s Deans Research Forum and at the UNC Charlotte Research Conference. In my free time I like to make short films concentrating on a variety of genres.

What are you looking forward to at the DNC: Everything! As a political science major I am fascinated with government and am excited to witness a major political event first hand. I am also looking forward to meeting government officials and politicians from across the country.

Why are you a part of the PPL: Of all the internships I came across the PPL offered the most hands on, creative, and interactive opportunity. The PPL combines both of my passions in life film and government. As an intern with the people I have the great opportunity of working with creative professionals, the local Charlotte community, and network with government officials. Being a part of the PPL will allow me to gain valuable experience that I can use in my future government career.

DNC chair to mark 100 days until convention

Posted by Corey Conner on May 25, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

DNC chair, Debbie Wasserman Schultz, will be at Bank of America Stadium on May 29 to mark 100 days until President Obama accepts the party’s nomination.

Shultz, along with the DNCC CEO, Steve Karrigan; Charlotte mayor, Anthony Foxx; and Durham resident, Karen Baldwin, will kickoff an effort to engage the people in the convention.

Photo Credit: Willamor Media

Media should park off of Graham Street in Lot 5. They should enter through the Lux Lounge entrance on Graham Street.

Event Details:

Where: Bank of America Stadium
Who: DNC Chair Debbie Wasserman Schultz
DNCC CEO Steve Kerrigan
Charlotte Mayor Anthony Foxx
Durham resident, Karen Baldwin

When: Tuesday, May 29.  Check-in 10:00 a.m., media availability begins at 10:30 a.m.

RSVP: RSVP to press@demconvention.com by 5:00 p.m. Monday, May 28.

Uber offers free ride for DNCC Spring Media Walkthrough

Posted by Corey Conner on May 24, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

There’s no need to stand on the street and hail a cab for the DNCC Spring Media Walkthrough. The DNCC and Uber, an app-driven private car service, have partnered up to offer transportation including a free first ride, up to $40.

Uber, available in the iPhone and Android app stores handles calling a driver, and  keeps the user’s credit card on file to take care of the tip.

Here’s the information on how to sign up:

To take your first ride, follow the steps below:

  1. ~5-10 minutes before you are ready to travel, open the app and move the pin to your location.  You can also text your address to 827-222 (e.g. 501 South College Street, Charlotte, NC 28202) or input it directly at m.uber.com.
  2. Submit your request and watch as your private driver is dispatched.  You will receive an estimate of when your driver will arrive and a text message when your driver is arriving.

Want more information on Uber in at the Spring Media Walkthrough?  Head to:  http://www.uber.com/dncc

Questions?  Email supportdncc@uber.com

 

 

CNN City Smackdown: 18 reasons on the last day to play

Posted by Desiree Kane on May 21, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Today is the last day to upload your stories to the CNN iReport City Smackdown! This is a great opportunity for anyone with a story to tell about Charlotte to get their video on national news. That’s killer media attention, why not capitalize on it?!? The only requirement is that it be about something you like about Charlotte.

I personally can think of at least 18 things/places/reasons to like Charlotte so if you’re hard up for ideas on what to make a video (to upload today!) about, here’s something to get you started:

  1. That doughnut smell Dilworth always has
  2. Freedom Park
  3. The public art gallery in NoDa
  4. Latta Plantation Park (yes, it’s technically in Huntersville)
  5. What good has become of the intersection at Freedom & W. Morehead
  6. The free CATS Gold Rush line
  7. The Ben F. Long Continuum fresco which randomly has Hugh McColl painted into it
  8. The Matheson Bridge Mural by William Puckett
  9. Lightning bugs
  10. The new Little Sugar Creek Greenway and Creek restoration
  11. Soil that is easy to grow amazing produce in
  12. That thing about Charlotte where if you really, genuinely need help someone will help you
  13. Compared to a lot of cities (not inferring anything about Tampa) Charlotte is extremely clean
  14. How almost everything is generally about 15-20 minutes away, max
  15. The big Carolina Farmers section of only local farmers and local foods at the farmer’s market on Yorkmont
  16. How we have gorgeous, pastoral views of the NC countryside 20 mins away in pretty much every direction
  17. Our blackberries in the height of the season will kick any other place’s blackberries’ booty
  18. Cool old architecture like the Excelsior Club on Beatty’s Ford, the Coca-Cola building in FreeMoreWest and Myrtle Square in Dilworth
Submit your story: http://ireport.cnn.com/topics/788285

In other, completely self serving news, here’s the Charlotte Video Project bit on what we’re up to that’s up on the CNN iReport page: http://ireport.cnn.com/docs/DOC-792138

Occupy movement looks toward political conventions

Posted by Justin Ruckman on Apr 25, 2012 in Blog | 0 comments

Chuck Burton/Associated Press

In planning protests this time around, for the conventions, social media is likely to play a bigger role.

“I think there’s been a lot more inter-occupation communication as people got away from the encampments and starting getting online and establishing lines of communication across the country,” said Domenic Battistella, 34, of nearby Mooresville, an original member of Occupy Charlotte. “We’re going to be much more coordinated on a regional basis.”

Occupy Charlotte members recently met with Occupy protesters from throughout North Carolina to prepare for the convention and are planning to provide housing, food and other support for those who come from out of state. An Occupy Durham member is now helping the legal team in a potential challenge as Charlotte officials continue to hold off allowing groups to apply for permits to protest at the convention.

Michael Zytkow, 25, an Occupy Charlotte member (…) hopes [an upcoming Bank of America] protest draws as many as 1,000 people, providing a test run for the convention and the city’s ability to control the demonstrations.

And how many will come for the convention?

“I’m guessing in the thousands upon thousands,” Mr. Zytkow said.
